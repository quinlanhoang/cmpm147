Experiment 4 - Infinite Worlds

Description

In this experiment, I was trying to make infinite, generative worlds.

Technical

All three sketches share the same global seed. They show the same seed and save across generation but differ in terms of displayment. They all have various depth to it in which the first world represents a 2D visualization of Minecraft plains with vibrant colors and a sin/cos wave in the water. World two represents the 3D visualization without Minecraft and is absent of color to make that monochromatic demo feel of what basic generation contrast is. World three is esentially the sketch and broken down etch of what our inspiration is based off of and still has leveled depth to create hills and declines for pools of water. Traversing and clicking on tiles will identify their coordinates and what kind of block they represent or could've been.

Reflection

Overall, I am content with the provided content. I think it really portrays the three levels of depth for ideation of such a kind of game and I believe that I can add more features later on such as block placement or animals roaming around. The hardest part was reiterating this into my portfolio as I ran into many different function errors and had to manually place sketch behind each variable that casued issues. Troubleshooting was rather exhaustive and I think I made a decent project for how much work & stress that went into it.
